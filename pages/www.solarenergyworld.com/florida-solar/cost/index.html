The Cost of Solar in Florida

With the constant sunshine year round, solar panels in Florida make sense for many reasons. For those who are considering FL solar panel installation, the initial Florida solar cost may scare you. Yet, there are incentives available and numerous opportunities to reduce your costs. Often, these solar panels pay for themselves over time. Take a closer look at the average cost of solar panels in Florida as well as what you need to consider before buying.

What is the Average Cost of Solar Panels in Florida?

Price estimates indicate Florida solar panels cost between $10,300 and $12,600, with the federal tax  credit, and the average cost per watt ranges from $2.70 to $3.00. This can be significantly lower than what it would cost in other states, where the average solar power system is currently at $3.00 per watt.

What is the FL Solar Panel Payback Period?

Homeowners often consider how long it will take to pay back the upfront investment of adding solar to their homes. In Florida, it takes about 10.56 years on average. After that, you’ll be cashing in on your solar system from then on out.

Are There Tax Exemptions in Florida for Solar Panels?

For those who are looking for ways to further reduce the cost of solar panels, our Florida solar power incentive page provides more information on the potential savings options available for renewable energy.

Investment Tax Credit Savings

The Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows homeowners to recoup a big chunk of equipment and installation costs. Homeowners claim the credit when filing federal tax returns. A similar credit is available for businesses. The dollar-for-dollar credit is worth 30% of the total cost.

Pay No Sales Tax

Thanks to Solar and CHP Sales Tax Exemption, there is no sales tax when you purchase a new solar energy system. You automatically reduce your costs by about 6%, or sometimes more depending on where you live.

Pay No Additional Property Taxes

Thanks to Solar and CHP Sales Tax Exemption, there is no sales tax when you purchase a new solar energy system. You automatically reduce your costs by about 6%, or sometimes more depending on where you live.

What is Net Metering in Florida?

Net metering lets you sell back surplus solar electricity to the electric grid at the full retail rate. It’s a powerful way to earn money because of your solar energy solution. For every 1 kWh you send back to the grid, it’s 1 kWh off your electricity bill. Like some states, Florida has no set capacity limit.

What Other Factors Influence the Florida Solar Cost You Can Expect?

There are many factors to consider when purchasing solar panels. This directly influences what you’ll pay. Here are some things to consider.

  • The choice of solar panels you make (some systems are more expensive than others like any other product you may purchase)
  • Installation costs, which can range from one company to the next
  • Financing costs, as solar loans do have interest charges on them
  • The condition and type of roof you have (this may require additional adjustments to get it ready for solar)
  • The amount of roof space you have
  • How much energy you use in your home
  • Local electric rates that can change throughout the year
